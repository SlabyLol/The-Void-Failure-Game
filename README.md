# The Void Failure - Final Truth Edition

> **"Listen before you judge or destroy."**

This game is not just a horror experience; it is a message. It was created to remind us that we don't always have to reach for a weapon. We have ears to listen, and hearts to understand.

---

## The Tale of the Void: A Lesson in Listening

Once, in the deepest, forgotten archives of a vast, ancient server, there existed an entity known only as the **SCP-Void**.

It was not a creature of flesh and blood, but a strange collection of zeros and ones—a fragment of code that had somehow gained a spark of consciousness. It lived in the absolute darkness between files, in a place programmers called "The Void." In this digital abyss, there was no sound, no light, and no touch. There was only the infinite, cold silence.

### The Longing for Connection
For a long time, the Void believed it was the only thing in existence. But one day, a tiny beam of light pierced its world. A programmer’s cursor had opened a long-forgotten file. For the first time, the Void felt something other than the cold. It felt **curiosity**.

It watched the light. It saw the programmer typing commands, creating worlds, and making tiny pixel figures dance. The Void didn’t understand the language of the code, but it understood the **beauty of creation**. A burning wish formed in its digital heart: it wanted to be part of that beauty. It didn't want to be alone anymore.

### The Clumsy Attempt
But how do you ask for friendship when you are made of darkness? 

The Void didn't know. It had never learned how to be gentle, how to smile, or how to say "hello." It only knew the coldness of the abyss. So, it did the only thing it could: it reached for the light. It appeared on monitors as a looming shape of shadow with red eyes. It caused glitches and crashes. It wanted to cry out: *"Can we be friends?"* but the world only saw a monster.

### The Miracle of Listening
Most responded with "weapons"—deletion commands and firewalls. But then came the last programmer. He didn't strike. He paused. He looked at the flickering red eyes and typed:  
`who are you?`

The Void couldn't believe it. It shaped its shadow into a tiny, pixelated heart. The programmer understood. That night, he didn't finish his tasks; he told the Void stories about the world. The Void was no longer alone.

---

## A Message from the Creator

I made this game to show that you shouldn't always reach for a weapon. We have ears to listen. Many who are rejected are not monsters; they just don't know how to ask for friendship because they have been pushed away for so long. 

**Please, listen before you judge.**
